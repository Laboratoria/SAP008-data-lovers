A little bit of Studio Ghibli's history

Studio Ghibli (スタジオジブリ) is an animation studio based in Koganei (Tokyo, Japan), founded on June 15, 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata, and producers Toshio Suzuki and Yasuyoshi Tokuma. They decided to create the studio after the success of the film “Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind” (Kaze no Tani no Naushika - 風の谷のナウシカ), animation that Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata released in 1984 by the animation studio Topcraft. In 1986, they released their first film, “Castle in the Sky” (Tenkü no Shiro Rapyuta - 天空の城ラピュタ). Studio Ghibli also collaborated with the visual development of various games such as Ni no Kuni, Ökami, Ori and the Blind Forest, etc. The company's logo is Totoro, a character from “My Neighbour Totoro” (Tonari no Totoro - となりのトトロ), animation released by Studio Ghibli in 1988. In 2001, a museum dedicated to the works of the studio was opened, it is called the Ghibli Museum and is based in Tokyo, Japan. It was Hayao Miyazaki himself who designed the architecture of the museum, using the same sketching techniques he used in his animations. To date, the studio has released 22 animations, has an animation in progress since 2016, called “How do You Live?” (Kimitachi wa Dou Ikiru ka - 君たちはどう生きるか), and has no release date.
